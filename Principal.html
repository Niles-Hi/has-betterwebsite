Principal's Message

Dear H.A.S. Family:
​
It is with great pleasure that I welcome you to the 2016 – 2017 school
year at Hsinchu American School (HAS). This is our 12th year of existence
and we are very excited about our many upcoming plans and initiatives.
​
We pride ourselves on offering a unique educational experience that delivers
a high degree of instruction in the math and sciences, but that also emphasizes
the importance of establishing a strong foundation in the liberal arts and the
benefits of experiential learning. Our program is created with the goal of shaping
our students into individuals who are prepared to succeed in the rapidly changing
landscape of the 21st century.
​
I am well aware of the importance of our position and being trusted with shaping the
valuable resource of young minds. I feel we have an excellent program that our dedicated
staff has worked rigorously toward creating and we are constantly improving.

This is my 5th year at HAS and I have seen incredible effort from the staff in that time.
This is our second year of being officially accredited with the Western Association of Schools
and Colleges (WASC). This is a big achievement and has been a school-wide initiative involving
many areas of improvement, including facilities (new library, offices, more electronics) and
academic improvements (strong curriculum, unit plans, selection and use of standards, developing
common assessments, and many more).

We also have a wide variety of new facilities that we are working on. We will have our
own sports facility this year, which will include a soccer field and a full-sized basketball
court that can also be used for volleyball. Additionally, we have expanded down one floor
in our middle school building and are opening a lot of student-focused rooms, such as a
student lounge and work area, a weight room, a room to practice musical performances, and
a middle school art room.

We are also promoting the use of RENWEB, which is a comprehensive Learning Management
System. This has allowed students and parents to track assigned homework, homework
submissions and grades. It has also resulted in much better communication between parents
and teachers.

We have many other initiatives we are working toward this year. We hope to promote greater
student involvement in extra-curricular activities, resulting in more active students with
multiple skill sets. We are also unveiling common assessments in all core subject areas.
Using the results of these assessments, and other data collected from sources such as student
work and parent and student surveys, we are planning on developing a comprehensive data
committee and system that will help track student progress and development and benefit us in
informing instruction. Finally, we are offering additional ESL courses in the middle school
and high school to help with English language learning.

I am very proud to be an HAS Wolf and am looking forward to a safe and productive year.
These are exciting times for everyone here and I am looking forward to sharing them with you all.

Sincerely,
Mr. Donnelly
Principal
